Raising The Score

An opportunity presented itself to me during my last semester of college: CARE Malawi, a branch of the World Health Organization, asked UNC's School of Media and Journalism to cover their efforts to improve maternal health care in rural Malawi. A team of about 30 UNC students and professors traveled to Africa with hundreds of pounds of recording equipment and dozens of laptops in tow.

For one week, we stayed in the village of Ntcheu, following the lives of Malawians affected by the work of CARE. While half the team created video stories out in the field, the other half designed and developed a website and graphics to house those stories. I created our logo and design style guide—and illustrated the website's panorama—around which the rest of the project molded.

What Would Have Been

This was the project's original name and wordmark until two weeks before the deadline. "MAYI" means "mother" in Chichewa, the native tongue of Malawians. The silhouette depicts a Malawian mother carrying a child on her back—inspired from a photo taken in Ntcheu by my former professor, Steven King.

A Last Minute Improvement

CARE Malawi requested we change the name of the project to reflect their method of quantifying their work—the Scorecard. The new logo combined elements of the Malawian flag with the silhouette of the mother on the move—to symbolize the ongoing progress being made to improve maternal health care.
